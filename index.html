First Impressions 🗓️

Perspectives 🤝

Embracing Our Differences

At first, I thought that whoever I married should be exactly like me—a sort of reflection. I hoped for someone who shared my practical mindset, my ambivert nature, and who also didn't drink 😜. I even figured that if I am occasionally quiet or a bit 'boring,' my partner should be the same way.

But recently, I had a realization: what is the point of having a life partner who is just a duplicate of myself? It is actually much more beautiful this way. Instead of a replica, I see the value in us bringing different perspectives to how we see things. We can balance each other, point out areas where we can improve, and grow together.

My Side 🧑

Practical, analytical, a bit quiet, and old-school in my commitments.

Your Side 👩

Practical, positive, and strong in standing for yourself. You bring new perspectives.

New Perspectives 💡

You bring a different way of seeing the world, adding colors and ideas I might overlook on my own.

Growth & Balance 🌱

I want to listen when you point out where I can improve, growing together as husband and wife.

Our Foundation 🏡

Comfort, Peace & Respect

If we walk this path together, my promise to you is simple: I will do my absolute best to make your life comfortable. In return, I hope you can bring peace and calm into mine. 🕊️

"I will respect your parents and family as my own, and I trust that you will do the same for mine. I want to understand your needs deeply, and I hope we can build a space where you understand mine too."

I also want to say that I truly respect how you know how to take a stand for yourself. That strength, combined with your positive attitude, is something I really admire and value in you. 🌟

We are human, and we might make mistakes. But my philosophy is straightforward: if I do something wrong, tell me directly and I will try my best to correct it. Likewise, if I feel something could be better, I hope you’ll hear my perspective with an open heart. 💞

Values 📜

A Bit Old School

I have always believed in the philosophy of being a "one-woman man." I want to dedicate my care, my effort, and my life to my wife. I know my thinking when it comes to marriage is a bit old-school—not about division of chores, but about commitment. I see myself spending my entire life with the same person. 💍

To be honest, the thought of this not working out is scary. And to make sure it does, I believe the single most important foundation is complete honesty.

People often advise not to disclose too much of yourself early on, warning that others might take you for granted, or see you as uninteresting. But I don't believe in playing those games. This is marriage. We should be able to show our true selves without fear. 🤝

Vulnerability 🗝️

Sharing the Quiet Moments

I am naturally shy and emotional, and I don't easily share how I feel with others. I've always had a habit of keeping things inside. My parents are incredibly supportive, but conversation is usually from a family perspective. Telling my mother about my worries or emotional stress just makes her take on too much load, and as guys, it's often hard to talk emotionally with parents. 😔

For a long time, I kept telling myself, "This is just how it is." But deep down, I always wished for someone to look at me, notice the silence, and simply ask, "Is everything okay?" 💭

I hope that we can be that safe space for each other—where I care for your comfort and needs, and you support me so that I always feel safe putting you first in everything. 🌸

To be completely honest, I don't trust people easily. It takes time for me to open up. But talking to you lately, I’ve felt a sense of comfort—I feel like I can truly trust you and your words. That is a very new and special feeling for me. 💚
